Conclusion

  1. Home
  • The ADHD-200 dataset represents a significant resource for this project to detect Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD). This large and diverse dataset which provides valuable participants physical characteristics data, was used to run decision tree algorithm to predict which ADHD category the participant belongs to i.e healthy control, ADHD combined (ADHD-C) type, or ADHD inattentive (ADHD-I) type.
  • ADHD is a heterogeneous disorder with varying symptoms and severity. The dataset facilitated research into identifying distinct behavioral patterns associated with different subtypes of ADHD, which can lead to more personalized treatment approaches.
  • The ADHD-200 dataset has contributed to a deeper understanding of the factors effecting ADHD. The relevant data visualization and correlation analysis provided helped to explore how 'IQ Measure', 'Verbal IQ', 'Performance IQ', and 'Full4 IQ' which are the measures of cognitive abilities are related to ADHD symptoms. By conducting anova test we could see that there is statiscally significant difference in Verbal IQ scores among the three ADHD diagnosis categories
  • One of the important insights is the strong positive correlation between Inattentiveness and impulsiveness features. It suggests that the ADHD patients with high inattentiveness, might have high impulsivity or one might lead to another.
  • Conducted effective feature selection using SelectKBest method to pick the best features leading to highest predictive power and accuracy
  • Finally, used decision trees and random forest algorithm to predict the ADHD category the participant belongs to. The decision tree yielded an an accuracy of 81 percent and random forest algorithm yielded 85 percent accuracy. This is obvious as random forest considers multiple decision trees.
  • However, the future scope for this project would be to consider data from sources other than ADHD-200 to make more accurate and wholistic predictions.
